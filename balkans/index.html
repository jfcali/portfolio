WESTERN BALKANS R+1

A brief analysis of the region's Science and Tech panorama

The Western Balkans involves several European countries, each of them with different economics, innovation policies, and distinct scientific/technological production.

The following set of insights are taken from data describing Research & Innovation activities of the region, as well as information about the actors involved in said projects.

A BIRD'S EYE VIEW

The data frames used report projects held across six countries: Serbia, Montenegro, Macedonia, Kosovo, Bosnia and Herzegovina and Albania, recording projects submitted to different grants and funds provided by the European Union across ten years, from 2007 and 2017.

KEY PLAYERS

Regarding key actors, University of Belgrade takes the lead, with 110 participations, accounting for more than 8.1% of total project participations. Three out of the top-5 organizations are from Serbia. Likewise, 72.8% of the total projects are made by only one participant, which where all excluded from the network graph.

EVOLUTION

Areas

Over ten years, activities have increased, with a lot of variation between periods, where 2014 outstands as the most varied with its neighboring years (2013 and 2015). Only 2009 and 2015 showed massive downward peaks, with the latest trend being a decrease as well, shown by a negative slope between 2015 and 2017.

In 2014, more projects labeled 'Protection and Security' and 'Cultural Heritage' where presented, covering space left by 'Social and economic transformations', 'Advanced computing and Big Data' and 'Next-generation Internet'. This was the only year in which 'Social and economic transformations' was not the leading area, showing a steady climb and leading numbers the rest of the years.

2014 also marked the definite shortage of 'Tools, technologies and digital solutions for health' projects, showing no participations on 2014 and only one in 2015 and 2016.
